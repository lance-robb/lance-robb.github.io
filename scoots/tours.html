Welcome to Comzumel Surfing!

Have you always wanted to learn how to surf? Allow surf champion, Nacho Gutierrez, and his professional team to get you started, or help advance you to that next level surf. We offer lessons for beginner and intermediate level surfers, as well as board rentals for the advanced level surfers. Regardless of your age or skill level, we have a surfing adventure that is just right for you.

Choose from our private lessons, couples lessons, family/small group lessons, or large/corporate group lessons. We even offer a great tour for bachelor-bachelorette parties! With safety being our first priority, our qualified, lifeguard and CPR certified instructors will teach you everything that you need to know in order to surf. We use real surfboards, not foam or plastic, so you will be surfing like the pros! As an exception, we will start the smaller children on foam boards in order to build their initial confidence.

Cozumel is the ideal place to kick start your surf career, with its soft rolling waves, turquoise water, and large undeveloped beaches on the “Other Side”. Cozumel Surfing is the best place to start! Our 20+ years of experience paired with our professionalism and topnotch service make us one of the best adventure tours you’ll ever experience. This is a one of a kind tour that will be the highlight of your vacation, and best of all, Nacho stands by his 20+ year guarantee that if you don’t stand up and surf, you don’t pay!

How To Drive Round Cozumel

Renting a car is a great way to get around the island independently and really enjoy all it has to offer.

A popular route is to take the highway south out of town, stopping at Discover Mexico and then continuing on to visit Chankanaab Beach Park or one of the other great beaches on the west coast.

Next, take the highway round the south part of the island, stop at Punta Sur if you have time, then drive north up the wild east coast - simply known locally as the Other Side. Stop at one of the many beautiful white sand beaches for lunch.

Spend the afternoon on the beach or on the drive back you could stop at San Gervasio, the Mayan Encounter or downtown for some shopping.

Punta Sur Park

Stunning white sand beach with reef for drift snorkeling and crystal clear water at Punta Sur Park ecological reserve.

Entrance to the reserve also includes the historic lighthouse, where you can climb up to see some amazing views, a small maritime museum and eco-bus transport to the beach.

A boat trip on the lagoon is also available for the chance to see crocodiles and other wildlife.

Chankanaab All-Inclusive Beach Tour

Everything you need for a fun-filled day at Cozumel's most famous beach park, including transport, entrance, sea lion show, snorkel gear and guide, buffet lunch and full access to all the facilities.

The snorkel guide at the beach will help fit you out with equipment and, if you ask and they are available, he or she will be pleased to show you the best spots to snorkel in the crystal clear protected Marine Park waters too.

You can relax on the large white sand beach and enjoy a full Mexican buffet lunch with drinks in one of the restaurants.

Don't miss the famous sea lion show either, when you arrive they will let you know the times of the performances.

Later you can learn about Mayan culture and history as you explore the jungle area with replica ruins. Complete with wooden huts where you can try corn tortillas hand-made using centuries old traditional techniques.

Become fascinated with what you learn on the eco-arqueological tour and stare deep into the turquiose blue lagoon.

You won't believe how much you can pack into one day in Cozumel!

Book a Cozumel rental now!

If you rent a vehicle whilst in Cozumel why not take a look at our island driving tips? From parking to bumps in the road, find some great advice in this quick guide.

  • Almost all downtown roads that run north-south, parallel to the seafront, have priority. Roads running east-west must yield.
  • Most downtown roads are one-way. Look for arrows on the street name signs, black and white arrow signs on corners or just check which direction cars are parked in.
  • Don't park on red curbs. It means no parking and the police may take your car or plates and fine you. If there is no red curb you still can't park if cars are parked on the other side of the street - 'double file' parking is not permitted downtown.
  • Beware of topes, the local speed bumps in the road. They are mainly downtown, but there are some on the roads around the island too. They're not always clearly marked and can be quite a shock to you and your car if you hit them at speed.
  • We've seen too many scooter accidents, even with experienced riders. So stay safe on vacation, don't rent a scooter unless you really understand the high risk you're taking. If you do, make sure you wear a good helmet, protective clothing and expect the unexpected.
  • Why close yourself inside an expensive air-conditioned rental when you can have real fun in an open top?!
  • Be careful at intersections with the new 4 lane highway on parts of the island. If you're not sure if you have right-of-way, proceed with caution!
  • When renting, pay the extra for comprehensive insurance! It's STRONGLY recommended.
  • Enjoy your vacation but please be responsible - drinking and driving is illegal and dangerous here too.
